What Are Stars?

Stars are huge, luminous spheres of hot gas that produce light and heat through nuclear fusion, just like the Sun. They are found throughout the universe and vary greatly in size, temperature, and brightness. Some stars are much larger and hotter than the Sun, while others are smaller and cooler.

More About Stars

Stars are formed in giant clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Over millions of years, gravity pulls the gas together, forming a dense core. When the core becomes hot enough, nuclear fusion begins, and a new star is born.

Stars in our Solar System

The Sun is a massive star located at the center of our solar system. It is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium gases and produces energy through a process called nuclear fusion. In this process, hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing enormous amounts of heat and light. This energy travels through space and makes life on Earth possible.The Sun’s gravity holds the entire solar system together, keeping the planets, asteroids, and comets in their orbits. It contains about 99.8 percent of the total mass of the solar system, making it by far the largest object in it. The surface of the Sun, known as the photosphere, has a temperature of about 5,500 degrees Celsius, while its core is much hotter, reaching millions of degrees.

When we look at the night sky, the stars we see are actually very far away, sometimes thousands or even millions of light years from Earth. The patterns formed by groups of stars are called constellations, which have been used for navigation and storytelling since ancient times.

More on Sun

The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old and is expected to continue shining for another 5 billion years. It provides the energy needed for photosynthesis, which supports life on Earth. Without the Sun’s heat and light, our planet would be cold and lifeless.The Sun is about 150 million kilometers away from Earth and is classified as a medium-sized star, also known as a yellow dwarf. Even though it appears small in the sky, it is actually about 109 times wider than Earth. The Sun’s gravity holds the entire Solar System together, keeping the planets, asteroids, and comets in their orbits.
