Galaxy?

Galaxies were formed billions of years ago after the Big Bang. Over time, gravity pulled matter together to form stars and galaxies. Even today, galaxies continue to move, evolve, and sometimes merge with one another.

Black holes and Galaxies

Most large galaxies contain a supermassive black hole at their center. In the Milky Way, this black hole is called Sagittarius A*. It has a mass millions of times greater than the Sun and influences the movement of stars near the galactic core.

Galaxies are Made Of

A galaxy is a massive system made up of:

  • Stars
  • Planets
  • Gas
  • Dust
  • Dark matter
  • Star clusters
  • Nebulae

All of these are held together by gravity.

Galaxies are the largest structures in the universe that are bound together by gravity.

Scientists Say...

Scientists estimate that there may be billions or even trillions of galaxies in the observable universe. Studying them helps us understand how the universe began and how it continues to change over time.

More on Galaxies

Galaxies are enormous systems made up of stars, planets, gas, dust, and mysterious dark matter, all bound together by gravity. They are the largest organized structures in the universe. Each galaxy can contain millions, billions, or even trillions of stars. Our own galaxy, the Milky Way, contains hundreds of billions of stars, including our Sun. When we look up at the night sky, the faint band of light we sometimes see is actually a small part of this vast galaxy seen from inside it.There are different types of galaxies in the universe, each with its own structure and appearance. Spiral galaxies have a flat, rotating disk with curved arms that wind out from a central bulge. These arms are rich in gas and dust, where new stars are constantly being formed. The Andromeda Galaxy is a famous example of a spiral galaxy
